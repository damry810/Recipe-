How to prepare Egusi Soup

Egusi soup is a popular Nigerian dish made with ground melon seeds (egusi), leafy greens, and meat or fish. Here's a basic recipe:  

    Ingredients:

  • Meat: 1 pound beef, goat meat, or smoked fish, cut into pieces
  • Leafy Greens:1 bunch of spinach or ugu leaves, washed and chopped
  • Egusi: 1 cup ground melon seeds
  • Palm Oil: 1 cup
  • Onions: 2 medium onions, chopped
  • Pepper:2-3 scotch bonnet peppers, blended
  • Crayfish:1/2 cup ground crayfish
  • Seasoning Cubes: 2-3
  • Salt:To taste
  • Water: As needed

Instructions:

    Prepare the Meat:

  1. Wash the meat and boil until tender. Remove the meat and set aside the stock.

    2. Make the Egusi Paste:

  2. In a bowl, combine the ground egusi, 1 chopped onion, and a little water. Mix until you have a smooth paste.

    3. Fry the Egusi:

  3. Heat the palm oil in a pot. Add the remaining chopped onion and fry until translucent. Add the blended pepper and fry for a few minutes. Add the egusi paste and fry, stirring constantly, until the oil separates from the egusi.

    4. Add the Stock:

  4. Pour in the meat stock and bring to a boil. Add the seasoning cubes, crayfish, and salt to taste.

    5. Simmer:

  5. Reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the egusi is cooked through.

    6. Add the Greens:

  6. Add the chopped leafy greens and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until they are wilted.

    7. Serve:

  7. Add the cooked meat to the soup and serve hot with your favorite Nigerian swallow (e.g., pounded yam, fufu, or eba).

Tips:

  • You can add other vegetables to your egusi soup, such as tomatoes, okra, or bitter leaf.
  • For a richer flavor, you can roast the egusi seeds before grinding them.
  • If you don't have ground egusi, you can grind the seeds yourself using a blender or food processor.
Enjoy your delicious and nutritious egusi soup!
